Research interests

I am broadly interested in the interface of geometry and physics.

I am interested in making explicit certain aspects of two early approaches to the geometric Langlands
correspondence: the first one by Drinfeld using Radon transform and the second by Beilinson-Drinfeld using
conformal field theories techniques. The moduli of Higgs bundles with its integrable structure provided by
the Hitchin fibration plays a key role in these formulations. The recent development in analytic geometric
Langlands often lurks behind as motivation for having a more explicit understanding of these early approaches.

I am also interested in the inherent geometric aspects of the moduli of Higgs bundles and related ones, such
as moduli of flat/projective connections on Riemann surfaces. Conformal limits, natural stratification and
deformation of symplectic structures on these moduli spaces are the usual ingredients I work with.

Recently, I have also developed interests in:

  • Donagi-Pantev-Simpson's approach to geometric Langlands and its relation to Drinfeld's approach;
  • Bridgeland's spaces of stability conditions and his complexification of moduli of Higgs bundles;
  • the interaction between random geometry (e.g. Schramm-Loewner evolution), real enumerative geometry
    and conformal field theories;
  • a geometric understanding of the Verlinde formula.
