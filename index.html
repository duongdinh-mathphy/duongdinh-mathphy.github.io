I am currently a postdoc scholar at Max Planck Institute for Mathematics in Bonn, Germany.

I completed my PhD in Mathematics (2023) and Master in Mathematical Physics (2019) at University of
Hamburg, under supervision of J. Teschner. Before that, I completed a dual bachelor degree in Math and
Physics at Korea Advanced Institute of Science and Technology (KAIST) in 2017.

Research interests

I am broadly interested in the interface of math and physics. My more specific interests often
revolve around the geometry of moduli spaces of Higgs bundles and holomorphic connections on Riemann
surfaces. The motivation often comes from the approaches to the geometric Langlands program using
techniques from conformal field theories, such as the one pioneered by Beilinson-Drinfeld in which
the quantization of the moduli of Higgs bundles plays a key role.

Recently, I am also interested in random geometry and its relation to conformal field theories.
